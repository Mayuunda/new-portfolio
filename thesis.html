Thesis

A bit of my reasoning

At the age of 14, I experienced the disastrous consequences of societal collapse firsthand. While "conflict" is a broad term, at its core, it is the violent clash of ideas in the absence of dialogue. It was a horrific reality—severe shortages of food and gas, armed violence in the streets, and a palpable sense that we had rapidly regressed as a society.

This experience did not spark an interest in conflict itself; rather, it crystallized a lifelong fascination with how technology dictates the outcome of warfare. From a young age, I was captivated by the sheer scale of WWII and the technological mobilization of the Manhattan Project. I studied the decimation of Europe, the rise of totalitarianism, and the unprecedented industrial efforts required by the Allies to secure victory. History shows us that hoping for peace is not a strategy. Just decades after WWII, the world saw Vietnam and Korea. Today, Eastern Europe is fighting the bloodiest war since 1945 against the Russian Federation.

Why does aggression persist from bad actors? Answering that is complex, but the reality is clear: we will never entirely eliminate the human propensity for conflict. What we can do is ensure we are so technologically prepared that we can decisively mitigate the aftermath of an attack, or end it swiftly.

This raises the ultimate question: can strategic technological supremacy prevent conflict altogether? This is the exact future I envision. We do not secure peace by disarming or halting defense innovation. We secure peace through strategic deterrence—by expanding our asymmetric advantage over our adversaries until the prospect of combating us becomes completely irrational. True security comes from possessing the undeniable capability to counter any offense.

The global threat matrix is expanding rapidly. There is no guarantee that Putin’s ambitions end in Ukraine, or that the conflict in the Middle East won't escalate further. Most critically, an annexation of Taiwan by China would immediately cripple the global semiconductor supply chain that underpins our entire modern infrastructure. We cannot ensure the safety of our way of life if bad actors hold the leverage. We must take action to maintain the absolute cutting edge of defense technology.

I am dedicating my life to solving arguably the toughest problem humanity has ever faced: securing peace through strength. I am a definite optimist. I believe the future holds incredible promise, but that future will not build or defend itself. I am stepping up to contribute to the making of it.

Extra Truth

What Interests Me

"

Since youth, I have been amazed by how quickly underlying disputes and radical changes in politics can fracture a society. I am often struck by the disconnect between diplomatic discourse and on-the-ground reality. Observing the bureaucratic inefficiency of global institutions like the UN and the cyclical polarization of domestic elections, it is clear that traditional systems of discourse are failing to prevent extremism, which made me question the legitimacy of political competence.

"

Interacting with a divided society that grows increasingly hostile against each other every four years during elections reveals how quickly polite academic engagement can transform into violence and hate. Moreover, I learned this not through theory, but through survival. Experiencing the collapse of a country undermined by political extremism and navigating the resulting chaos in Peru, being physically compromised amid the unrest, taught me a harsh reality: when systems of equity and communication break down, the human cost is immediate.

We have stepped far away from systems of equity and union, and we require exhaustive efforts of reintegration and reconnection between people. I cannot help but wonder how we can use software to engineer intelligent platforms that identify vulnerabilities before they fracture, creating the technological infrastructure needed to protect communities when traditional institutions fail. This is how we cut through chaos, secure vulnerable networks, and provide the clarity needed to bridge our deepest divides.
